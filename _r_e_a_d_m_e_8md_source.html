14 A Wick contraction in this context means the analytic result obtained when
15 "integrating out" fermionic degrees of freedom from the path integral in
16 lattice QCD. Therefor it builds the bridge between the results of the numerical
17 integration and the physical observables as described in section sec:intro
18 by computing the euclidean correlator.

21 In the sLapH framework space is approximated by the span of the eigenvectors
22 of a Laplace operator, below referred to as eigenvector space. Introducing
23 random vectors and dilution in time, eigenvector and Dirac space yields a
24 stochastical estimate for the propagator.
25 The correlation function can be expressed as a trace of products of
26 propagators, eigenvectors and field operators (@f$\Gamma@f$).

28 In the framework of the LapHs-method, the quark lines could be expressed by
29 the @f$ V_s @f$ whose dimension was the number of LapH eigenvectors. Thus for the
30 LapHs-method it is possible
31 to only apply the random vectors in the LapH-subspce spanned by spin, time
32 and LapH eigenvector number. To distinguish these random vectors from the
33 usual ones, the are denoted by @f$ \rho @f$. For the Quark lines this results in

40 To illustrate these rather abstract concepts, the example of a single charged
41 pion shall be discussed. Its quantum numbers are @f$ J^P = 0^- @f$, a
42 pseudoscaler particle. The operator with the largest overlap to the
43 charged pion is
44 @f{align}{
45  O_{\pi^+}(n) = u(n) \gamma_5 \bar{d}(n).
46 @f}
47 The Operators in the euclidean correlator are then chosen to be a pion
48 creation operator @f$ O_{\pi^+}^\dagger(n) @f$ and a pion annihilation
49 operator @f$ O_{\pi^+}(m) @f$ at two different lattice points $m$, $n$.
50 Inserting this into the path integral eq. {eq:correlatorlqcd}
51 the fermionic part is a Grassmann integration that can be carried out
52 analytically by performing a Wick-contraction. It will be denoted as the
53 fermionic expectation value @f$ \langle \rangle_F @f$.

193 For full dilution of the dirac-index @f$ \mathbf{B} @f$ becomes blockdiagonal
194 in dirac space. Writing the euclidean correlator in matrix-vector notation
195 the product @f$ \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{B} @f$ occurs. At this point the
196 blockdiagonal structure can be exploited. Rather than a multiplication
197 in which twelve of the sixteen blocks of @f$ \mathbf{B} @f$ are zero, it suffices
198 to perform matrix multiplications for the four nonzero blocks.
199 
200 The code I implemented exploits this feature to save the effort of the
201 mulitplication @f$ D^{-1} \cdot \Gamma @f$. To illustrate the idea, I want to
202 perform an example calculation.
203 
204 Let w.l.o.g
205 @f{align}{
206  \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{B} &=& \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 & 7
207  \\ 8 & 9 & 10 & 11 \\ 12 & 13 & 14 & 15 \end{pmatrix} \cdot
208  \begin{pmatrix} A & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & B & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & C & 0 \\
209  0 & 0 & 0 & D \end{pmatrix} \\
210  &=& \begin{pmatrix} 0A & 1B & 2C & 3D \\ 4A & 5B & 6C & 7D \\
211  8A & 9B & 10C & 11D \\ 12A & 13B & 14C & 15D \end{pmatrix}
212 @f}

214 Here, @f$ \{0, \dots, 15\} @f$ denote blocks of size number_of_eigen_vec
215 @f$ \cdot @f$ number_of_dilution_E and @f$ \{A, \dots, D\} @f$ blocks of size
216 number_of_dilution_E @f$ \cdot @f$ number_of_eigen_vec. Performing
217 @f$ D^{-1} \cdot \Gamma @f$ culminates in reordering columns of eqn.

268  GlobalData processes them to vectors of certain structs for Quarklines,

271  Internally all physical loops are replaced with auto-loops over these

279  quarklines are constructed in LapH::Quarklines.
